Latest C.B.S.E Updates

New National Education Policy (NEP) 2023

Overview

  • Structure Change: Replaces the 10+2 education system with a 5+3+3+4 structure for a more holistic approach.
  • Launch Date: Approved by the Union Cabinet on July 29, 2020.
  • Objective: Aims to transform the education system for comprehensive, flexible, and high-quality learning.

Key Features

  1. Age of Schooling: Extended from 6-14 to 3-18 years, including 3 years of pre-schooling.
  2. Medium of Instruction: Emphasizes native languages until at least Grade 5.
  3. Higher Education Commission of India (HECI): Replaces UGC, AICTE, NCTE for streamlined governance of higher education.
  4. Blurring of Streams: No formal distinctions between arts, science, and commerce.
  5. Flexible Undergraduate Degrees: 3 or 4 years with exit options; Academic Bank of Credit introduced.
  6. Common Access to School Education: Ensures universal access and holistic development.
  7. Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN): Aims for FLN by grade 3 by 2025.
  8. Experiential Learning: Emphasis on practical learning, critical thinking, and reduced course content.
  9. Graduate Enrollment Ratio: Aims to increase to 50% by 2035 from 26.3% in 2018.
  10. Open and Distance Learning Standards: Initiatives to enhance standards and recognition.
  11. Technological Innovation: Introduction of National Educational Technology Forum (NETF) for technology integration.
  12. Internationalization of Education: Encourages global collaborations and allows top world universities to open campuses in India.
  13. Public Investment: Aims for a 6% GDP investment collaboration between Centre and State governments.

Objective of NEP 2023

Overhauling the education system to address past pitfalls, emphasize practical skills, and bridge gaps between vocational and formal education. Focused on Access, Equity, Quality, Affordability, and Accountability. Aims to make India a global knowledge superpower.

Creation of NEP

Formulated by a panel led by former ISRO chief K Kasturirangan. Proposals gathered and approved by the Ministry of Education.

Transformation of Indian Education System:

Traced from the 1st commission in 1948 to the passage of NEP in 2020.

NEP 2023 Highlights

  • Extends compulsory schooling from age 3-18.
  • Emphasizes mother tongue as the medium of instruction.
  • Establishes HECI for higher education governance.
  • Blurs distinctions between arts and sciences.
  • Offers flexible undergraduate degrees with exit options.
  • Focuses on universal access, experiential learning, and technological innovation.
  • Encourages internationalization of education and increases public investment in education.
